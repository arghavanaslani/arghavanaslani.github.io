## Overview

The canonical textbook for computational neuroscience. Covers neural encoding and decoding, information theory, network models, plasticity, and reinforcement learning — all with rigorous mathematical treatment.

Not an easy read, but returning to chapters as needed is more useful than reading cover to cover.

## Chapters I keep coming back to

- **Ch. 7 — Network Models**: The section on excitatory-inhibitory balance and stability is foundational for understanding attractor dynamics.
- **Ch. 10 — The Kalman Filter and Optimal Estimation**: Bridges signal processing and Bayesian inference in a way that clicked for me.

## Passages worth keeping

> "Neural responses are not just descriptions of the world; they are descriptions of the world as filtered through the needs and architecture of the organism."

## Notes

Works best alongside actual papers — use it as a mathematical reference when you encounter a concept in the literature that needs unpacking.
