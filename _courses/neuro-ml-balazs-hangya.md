## Overview

A lecture series that walks through applying ML methods to neuroscience data: from basic regression and classification through to deep learning for neural decoding. Particularly useful for understanding how to move between theory and experimental data.

## What I found most useful

- The sessions on dimensionality reduction (PCA, t-SNE, UMAP) applied to population activity recordings
- The discussion of cross-validation and overfitting as it applies to neural data, which has different structure to typical ML datasets
- Practical code examples in Python/MATLAB

## Notes

The section on Gaussian processes was dense — worth watching twice. The connection to Bayesian methods is made explicit and is helpful context for reading computational papers.
